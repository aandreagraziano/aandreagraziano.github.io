About me

    My research interest lies at the intersection of Theoretical Neuroscience and Machine Learning, in exploring neural principles to build biologically-plausible computational models that support adaptive behavior and flexible learning. I believe this is crucial for enhancing the utility of future artificial intelligence as well as ensuring a responsible development of next-generation AI systems.

    Right now, I'am a Research Intern at Institute for Neural Computation, Ruhr University Bochum. My current research focuses on Clone-structured-causal-graphs (CSCGs), as presented in [1] and [2]. Ùnder the supervision of , my goal is to provide a better understanding of how CSCGs support causality by validating the cloned Hidden Markov Model (HMM) against counterfactual causal criteria. This is useful to demonstrate that the model can serve as a computational implementation of the episodic memory theory developed by Markus Werning, proposing that episodic memory play a crucial role in causal reasoning through counterfactuals.
