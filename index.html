About me

    I am a Researcher in Artificial Intelligence at Vicarious: we build an AI layer for robots by combining generative probabilistic models with cognitive science insights. Passionate about applying human cognition bias to machine learning, I am convinced a data-efficient, model-based, neuroscience-inspired approach is required for building "intelligent" systems which generalize from a limited number of training examples.

    Prior to joining Vicarious, I graduated from MIT Operations Research Center Master of Science, where I was advised by Prof. Rahul Mazumder. My thesis lies at the intersection of Machine Learning, Optimization and Statistics, building new algorithms to compute interpretable estimators, and understanding their statistical performance. I earned an MS in Applied Mathematics from Ecole Polytechnique.

    I worked as Machine Learning research intern in the Equity Derivative Structuring team of Societe Generale. In addition, I gained two international experiences as a Web Developer in Option, Santiago (Chile) and Teacher Assistant in Shanghai ParisTech Jiao Tong (China).

    I am a movie-fan, a sports addict, and a world-traveller. As an amateur photograph, I share my journeys around on my website.
